1 The Hanging Gardens of Babylon are one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. However, unlike the pyramids of Egypt, no one has ever found evidence that they actually existed. For more than two thousand years, people have wondered whether these legendary gardens were real or just a beautiful story.
2 According to ancient writers, the gardens were constructed around 600 BCE by King Nebuchadnezzar II. He built them as a gift for his wife, Amytis, who missed the green mountains of her homeland. The gardens were supposedly built on terraces that rose high into the air, making them look like they were hanging from the sky.
3 Ancient historians described an amazing irrigation system that brought water from the Euphrates River up to the highest levels of the garden. Slaves worked in shifts, turning wheels and pulling chains to lift the water. This would have been an incredible engineering achievement for that time.
4 Modern archaeologists have searched for the gardens in the ruins of ancient Babylon, near present-day Baghdad, Iraq. They have found impressive walls and buildings, but nothing that matches the descriptions of the famous gardens. Some experts now believe the gardens may have been located in a different city altogether.
5 Whether real or imaginary, the Hanging Gardens continue to capture our imagination. They represent humanity's dream of creating beauty in the midst of a harsh desert. Perhaps the mystery of their existence makes them even more fascinating than if we knew exactly where they once stood.
